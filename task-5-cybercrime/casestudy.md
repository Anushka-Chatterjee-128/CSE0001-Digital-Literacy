# Case Study: UPI Payment Fraud via Online Marketplace

**The Crime:** UPI fraud involves tricking an individual into authorizing a payment from their bank account under the false pretense of receiving money. 

**The Scenario:**
Rahul, a first-year college student, listed his old engineering drawing board for sale on a local university Facebook group. Within an hour, a "buyer" contacted him on WhatsApp, agreeing to the asking price without any negotiation. To build trust, the scammer even sent a photo of a fake university ID card. 

**Step-by-Step Execution:**
1. **The Hook:** The scammer claimed they were out of town and wanted to pay immediately via UPI to reserve the drawing board.
2. **The Trap:** The scammer told Rahul they were using a "merchant account" and sent a QR code to Rahul's phone. 
3. **The Deception:** The scammer instructed Rahul to scan the QR code and enter his UPI PIN, falsely claiming this was necessary "to authenticate the account and receive the funds."
4. **The Theft:** Believing he was authorizing a deposit, Rahul entered his PIN. 

**Typical Targets:** College students trying to make a quick buck, the elderly, or anyone who is unfamiliar with the specific mechanics of digital payments. 

**The Consequences:**
Instead of receiving ₹1,500, that exact amount was instantly deducted from Rahul's bank account. The scammer immediately blocked his number. Rahul lost a significant portion of his monthly pocket money and learned a harsh lesson about digital trust, leaving him feeling violated and financially stressed.
