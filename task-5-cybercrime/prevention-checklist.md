# 'Stay Safe Online' Checklist for College Students

Navigating the digital world on campus can be tricky. Use this checklist to protect your digital identity and your wallet.

* **1. Remember the Golden Rule of UPI:** You *never* need to enter your UPI PIN to receive money. Your PIN is strictly for sending money or checking your bank balance. If someone asks you to enter your PIN to "receive a refund" or "get a payment," it is a scam.
* **2. Verify before you pay:** Always double-check the receiver's name that pops up on your screen before hitting send, especially when scanning a QR code at a local college canteen or clicking payment links sent via SMS or WhatsApp.
* **3. Beware of the "Urgent Friend" scam:** If a friend messages you out of the blue on Instagram or WhatsApp asking for emergency money, call them directly on their phone number to verify. Their account has likely been hacked.
* **4. Avoid public Wi-Fi for sensitive transactions:** Never log into your banking app or make online purchases while connected to the free campus, library, or cafe Wi-Fi. Always switch to your mobile data for financial transactions.
* **5. Lock down your social media:** Don't overshare. Keep your exact hostel room number, daily schedule, and live location private. Scammers use these small details to build trust or answer your security questions.
* **6. Think before you click:** Beware of messages in college WhatsApp groups offering "free premium accounts," unbelievable discounts, or guaranteed remote internships. If it sounds too good to be true, it’s a phishing link designed to steal your credentials.
* **7. Use strong, unique passwords & 2FA:** Stop using your student ID, your pet's name, or your birthday as your password. Enable Two-Factor Authentication (2FA) on your email, WhatsApp, and social media accounts so hackers can't get in even if they have your password.
* **8. Keep your software updated:** Don't ignore those annoying update notifications. Always install the latest OS and app updates on your phone and laptop, as they contain vital security patches against new malware.

### 🚨 How to Report Cybercrime in India
If you or a friend falls victim to an online scam, act immediately:
* **Call the National Cybercrime Helpline:** Dial **1930** immediately to report financial fraud and potentially freeze the transaction.
* **File an Official Complaint:** Visit the National Cyber Crime Reporting Portal at **[cybercrime.gov.in](https://cybercrime.gov.in/)** to register a formal complaint.
