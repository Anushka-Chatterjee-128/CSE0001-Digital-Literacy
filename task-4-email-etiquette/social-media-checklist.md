The Do's ✅



* Do curate a professional presence: Use platforms like LinkedIn to showcase your academic projects, skills, and extracurricular achievements.
* Do review your privacy settings: Regularly check who can see your posts and tag you in photos across all your personal accounts.
* Do think before you post: Apply the "front page rule"—if you wouldn't want it on the front page of a newspaper (or seen by a recruiter), don't post it.
* Do network actively: Follow industry leaders, university organizations, and alumni in your field to stay informed and build connections.
* Do set boundaries for screen time: Take regular digital detoxes to protect your mental health and maintain focus on your studies.



The Don'ts ❌



* Don't overshare sensitive information: Avoid posting your exact location, daily schedule, phone number, or student ID online.
* Don't post unprofessional content: Avoid sharing photos or videos involving illegal activities, heavy partying, or offensive language.
* Don't vent about academic or work issues: Never complain publicly about your professors, classmates, or current/former employers.
* Don't engage in cyberbullying or trolling: Avoid getting dragged into heated, toxic arguments in comment sections.
* Don't let social media disrupt your routine: Avoid mindless scrolling when you should be studying, attending lectures, or sleeping.
